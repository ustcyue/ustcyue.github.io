Projects

P-EFIT (2010-2013): A program used for fusion plasma equilibrium reconstruction developed by me.
  • In order to fullfill the real-time control requirment, the program was written with CUDA-C. It could complete one compute iteration in 220 micorsecond and was the fastest equilibrium reconstruction program heretofore.
  • Plasma equilibrium is a kind of program used to identify plasma shape in the nuclear fusion device. It is the basis for fusion plasma shape control. But it conatins a series of complex computation, both numerically and logically, which make its speed much slower compared to the ideal spead for efficient feed-back control. It was originally defined as "non-parallelizable" and all previous attempts trying to parallelize it had failed.
  • P-EFIT successfuly accelerate this process by more than 10 times while improving its spatial resolution by four times.To achieve this, I designed several parallel algorithms in order to make full use of GPU resources. Including parallel block-tridiagonal solver(with DST method), tri-diagonal solver, efficient algorithm for middle-scale(500-5000) matrix multiplication(two examples here), etc. More importantly, these algorithms were carefully optimized based on property of GPU architechture and I had built an efficient data transfer scheme with two host threads, and three buffers.
  • I had been doing this project from July, 2010 to June, 2013 in Computer Engineering Division, Institute of Plasma Physics, Chinese Academy of Science. You can find more information about this project in my publications and my master's thesis(in Chinese).
  • I successfully got a 200000yuan (about $33000) Nature Science Foundation of China in 2012 for this research project. I was also awarded the national scholarship for graduate student for this work.

PBW for CSNS (2009): Designed the Proton Beam Window for China Spallation Neutron Source.
  • PBW is an important component at the end of High Energe Proton Accelerator. It is used to seperate the accelerator chamber(extremely vacuum) with the target room(full of helium gas, two barometric pressure). It had to suffer the impact of 1.6GeV proton beam. What's more, it should be as thin as possible so that protons would not loss much energe after passing it.
  • All exisited spallation neutron souces use water-cooled PBW. After carefully analysising and caculation based on the specific situation of CSNS(energy, beam phase, etc), I proposed a scheme that uses the helium gas in the target chamber to cool the PBW. In this way, the amount of high radiation water is reduced. Most importantly, less energy will loss when protons pass this window.
  • To verify its feasibility, I wrote a program in Fortran to simulate the process using finite-element method. It was the first time I wrote a practical program. When I finally got my program work correctly after developing and debugging for several weeks, I was amazed by the sense of accomplishment it brought to me. It felt like I had built a PBW in the virtual world with those codes. This is why I turned into CS.
  • I was awarded Outstanding Undergraduate Research Program of USTC for this program.
